Reading comes first

School teaches students to answer mathematics. We teach them to read it.

Students are asked to read mathematics long before anyone teaches them how. So we begin there, with the definitions, diagrams and proofs they are usually rushed past, and we slow down until each one makes sense.

It is the step most teaching skips. It is the one we start with.

The curriculum

Three courses. One path.

Every student begins with Read Like a Mathematician. From there the path continues, into writing, and then into thinking for oneself. Each course stands on its own. Taken together they are a single education, and the student who follows all three learns not simply to do mathematics, but to think as a mathematician does.
