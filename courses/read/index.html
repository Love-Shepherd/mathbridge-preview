Why this matters

You already think of yourself as a maths person.

You’re good at this. Numbers make sense to you, and in class you’re usually the one who gets there first.

Then you meet a definition that doesn’t quite click, or a proof that moves faster than you can follow, and for a moment that feeling disappears. It isn’t because you’re less capable than you think. It’s because nobody ever taught you how to actually read mathematics. Only how to calculate it.

Most classrooms move fast, because there’s a syllabus to finish. A definition gets half a sentence. A proof is shown once, then it’s on to the next topic. Even strong students end up far better at calculating than at understanding, and that gap tends to catch up with you later, not sooner.

Introducing

Read Like a Mathematician

Eight weeks, one to one, starting from the very first idea.

This course exists to close that gap. Over eight weeks, one to one, we go back to the definitions, symbols, diagrams and proofs you’ve been rushed past, and we stay with each one until it actually makes sense. Not just until you can repeat it back.

This isn’t about falling behind and catching up. It’s about learning mathematics properly, the way it should have been taught from the start.

The course

How the course works

I

One hour a week, one to one

Every session is led personally by Lerato, one to one, online, one hour a week. There’s no group to keep up with, and no question is ever too small to ask.

II

No new mathematics

You won’t learn new mathematics in this course. You’ll learn to properly read the mathematics you already know, the definitions, the notation, the examples and the proofs, until none of it is a mystery anymore.

III

From symbols to proofs

You start by learning to read a line of symbols as a real sentence. From there, you learn to test a definition, question an example, and take a proof apart until you can see exactly why it works. By the end, you can pick up a page of mathematics you’ve never seen before and work out what it says, on your own.

IV

The portfolio

The final two weeks are different. You gather everything you’ve made across the course and build a portfolio of your own mathematical reading, in a form you choose yourself.

Who this is for

Who this is for

Read Like a Mathematician is for students taking the IB Diploma, AS Level or A Level.

It suits you if you’re willing to ask questions, even the ones that feel obvious, and if you want to actually understand your mathematics. Not just get through the next test.
