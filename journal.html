The Rooted Mathematician.

The journal and newsletter of MathBridge. I write here about mathematics, education, books and the ideas that shape me, as a writer and a scholar.

Some essays begin with a theorem. Others with a sentence, a book or a question that refuses to let me go. They are for readers who enjoy ideas that stay with them.

The Oldest LanguageMathematics is the oldest language we have. Beginning with a notched bone older than writing itself, this essay traces how a human inheritance came to be read, and why it belongs to everyone.
Why Good Students Struggle at UniversityUniversity mathematics asks students to learn in new ways. The transition is often harder than they expect.
The Student Who No Longer Needs MeThe purpose of teaching is not to create dependence. It is to help students become capable of learning for themselves.
Reading Beyond the SyllabusSome of the ideas that shape us most never appear in an examination paper. They are worth encountering all the same.
