From the founder

The first year of Higher Level

Most students choose Analysis and Approaches Higher Level because mathematics has always come easily to them. I love that confidence. I also know what the first term can do to it.

HL Mathematics has a reputation for being the most demanding subject in the Diploma. Classes that start the year with ten students often end it with six. Often, the issue is the pace. A topic is introduced, examined and left behind within a few weeks, and the practising is expected to happen at home.

What changes is not the student. It is what mathematics asks of them. Proof arrives with nothing before it: no IGCSE or MYP topic prepares you to write an argument rather than an answer, and marks are lost for a conclusion that is merely implied. Complex numbers hand you an entirely new number system. Vectors, the longest topic in the syllabus, sits in every examination paper and chains four or five techniques into a single question. Ask HL graduates which topic shook their confidence, and vectors is the answer you hear most.

Introducing

HL Enrichment for DP1

Eight weeks ahead of the syllabus.

This programme gives students entering DP1 their first encounter with the topics that most often unsettle Higher Level students, before the syllabus reaches them formally. When these topics arrive in class, the student meets a second impression rather than a first shock.

The choice of topics is deliberate. Each one is either completely new at Higher Level, so nothing from earlier study can help, or known for needing more than one pass before it settles. Proof comes early because the rest of the course leans on it. Vectors gets the most time because it needs it.

The four topics

What we cover

Complex numbersA new number system: the Argand diagram, polar and Euler form, De Moivre’s theorem and the roots of unity.
ProofDirect proof, proof by contradiction and proof by induction: learning to write an argument, not just an answer.
Sequences, series and the extended binomial theoremSigma notation, arithmetic and geometric series, the sum to infinity, and expansions for negative and fractional powers.
VectorsLines and planes in three dimensions: intersections, angles and distances.

Each week holds two sessions. In the lesson, I introduce and develop the new material through worked examples. In the tutorial, the four of us sit with a problem set and work it together, so the week’s ideas settle before the next topic begins.

Who this is for

This is for the student who intends to stay.

The programme suits a student entering DP1 who has chosen Higher Level and means to keep it. It is not remedial, and it is not tutoring that chases the school’s homework. It runs ahead of the syllabus, not behind it.

Cohorts hold four students. A tutorial with four people is close work: every proof is read, every misreading is caught, and nobody watches from the back.
