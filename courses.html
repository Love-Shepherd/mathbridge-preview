Where to begin

MathBridge now meets you in more than one way.

Not every student arrives needing the same thing, so MathBridge now offers more than one door in. Some of you want a clear picture of where you stand. Some already know you need to properly learn to read mathematics, one to one. Others want to meet the hardest ideas of the IB Diploma before the classroom does. Four ways in, each explained below.

Begin here

The Portrait

You want a clear picture of where you stand before deciding what comes next. The Portrait exists for that.

Over two short sittings, about ninety minutes in total, you work through questions built to show how you actually think, not how many marks you lose. Whether you read a question before reaching for a method. Whether you trust a definition over instinct.

There is no score and no label. You receive a written assessment addressed to you personally. Parents receive a shorter letter with our recommendation and what it would cost.

The Portrait costs R1 500, credited in full against any one to one course that follows. Sometimes the right answer is no course at all, and we say so.

Course I

Read Like a Mathematician

Most classrooms move quickly, because there is a syllabus to finish. A definition gets half a sentence, a proof is shown once, and the class moves on before either has properly settled. This course is the opposite of that. Over eight weeks, one to one, we return to the definitions, notation, diagrams and proofs you have usually only half met, and we stay with each one until it genuinely makes sense to you. Not until you can repeat it back, but until you could explain it to someone else.

The learning experience

Every lesson is taught personally, by Lerato.

Lessons are built around real conversation, close reading and working through problems side by side. You keep your own notes, return to the ideas that matter most, and slowly grow confident enough to meet unfamiliar mathematics without needing anyone beside you. That growing independence is the whole point.

The full path

Read, then write, then think.

A MathBridge education is meant to be taken in full. Each course prepares the ground for the next, and together the three form a single progression rather than three separate purchases. Complete all three and you leave not only stronger at mathematics, but a young scholar, ready for university and for work that is genuinely your own.

Think Like a MathematicianAn independent inquiry into a question you choose, with original work to show for it at the end.Twelve weeks, the final stage

We do not price Write and Think as separate purchases, because they were never meant to be taken alone. Once you are settled into Read, we will talk with you about what continuing looks like, and what it costs.

In a group

The Prologue

Not everyone wants one to one teaching, and not everyone needs it. If you are beginning the first year of the IB Diploma in Mathematics Analysis and Approaches Higher Level, The Prologue meets complex numbers, proof, sequences, series and vectors, the topics that unsettle Higher Level students most, before the syllabus does. Taught in a small group of six, so that when these ideas arrive in the classroom, you are meeting them for the second time, not the first.
