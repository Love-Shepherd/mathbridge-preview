Our philosophy

Mathematics is a language.

We do not mean this as a metaphor. We mean it precisely.

It has a vocabulary, a grammar, a literature. Like any language, it can be read, written and thought in. No one is born fluent.

The half we rarely teach

We teach literature by teaching students to read closely and write with care. We rarely teach mathematics that way. It is taught as procedure, seldom as a language. That is the half we teach.

On fluency

Fluency is not a gift. It is the work of attention and time.

From school to university

At school, mathematics rewards speed. At university, it rewards understanding. The students who flourish are the ones who learned to read.

Where it begins

Mathematics began everywhere. It is not the inheritance of one civilisation, but a human inheritance that belongs to your child as fully as to anyone. Among the oldest mathematical objects in the world is the Lebombo bone, carved in Southern Africa some forty-three thousand years ago.

Why now

Calculation now belongs to machines. Reading a problem closely, judging when an answer is wrong, making an argument another mind can trust: these remain ours. They are acts of language.

This is not tutoring. It is an education in how to think.
