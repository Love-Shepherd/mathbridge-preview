The programme

Three programmes, one path.

Mathematics is learned the way a language is learned: in sequence, and in good company.

i · Read Mathematics  —  Six weeks

Before anyone can write or think in a language, they must be able to read it. We slow down to the level of the single line and learn to take it apart with patience. A page that once closed itself opens.

ii · Write Mathematics  —  Six weeks

To write mathematics is to make an argument another person can follow, and believe. Students learn to choose each word and symbol on purpose, and to build reasoning that holds.

iii · Think in Mathematics  —  Twelve weeks · Flagship

The capstone, and the point of everything before it. We meet once a week. The second meeting belongs to the students, who work through hard problems together, as peers. By design, they need me less as the weeks go on. They leave able to think in the language on their own. It is the truest preparation for university, and for the work that follows.

How a cohort works

Each programme meets twice a week, live and online. One session to learn a method, one to put it to work. Every student keeps the recording and a set of written notes. Read and Write are the foundations, and both come before Think in Mathematics.

What you leave with

There are no tests. Students build a portfolio: a living document of how they think, in their own hand, which they can carry into a university application and keep long after.

Small cohorts, by application only

We take eight students to a cohort, and admit them by application. Cohorts are composed by stage, so each student learns beside true peers.
